Venue & Travel

Information for reaching the Monte Sant'Angelo campus and the conference venue in the historic center.

Monte Sant'Angelo Campus

Department of Mathematics and Applications "R. Caccioppoli"

Naples, Italy

Note that the University of Naples is scattered across several locations in the city. The Department of Mathematics is in the Monte Sant'Angelo campus.

Public transport is run by several operators, and even the Metro lines are run by different companies. You will typically need a biglietto integrato which is valid everywhere except for the Alibus (e.g. the €1.80 single 'integrato' is valid for 90 minutes within city limits).

Moving Around Naples

The weekly integrated ticket (€16) is the easiest way to move around Naples. It is valid on all means of transport (bus, metro, suburban trains, etc) within the city limits for 7 days. The Alibus airport shuttle is not included.
There are several apps, such as ANM GO and MooneyGO, to help you plan trips, check what ticket you need, and even buy digital QR tickets for all companies serving Naples.
Moovit (most lines) and Gira Napoli (ANM buses only) can show you the realtime location of trains, metro, and buses.
The 'Tap & Go' system is only available on certain lines and it is inactive on most buses. We recommend you buy tickets instead.
Please remember that integrated tickets are valid on all lines; normal tickets are only valid with the company you bought them from and typically do not include connections. You can incur substantial penalties if you travel with the wrong ticket.
Integrated tickets can be bought digitally with the above apps, or physically at authorized resellers of the Unico Campania circuit, ANM Points, and ticket machines inside metro line 1, 6, 11 and funicular stations.

Airport & City Center

From the city center: use a Metro line up to Piazzale Tecchio (drop off at Campi Flegrei for Line 2 or Mostra for Lines 6, 9), followed by buses S1, S2, R6, or 180 towards Monte Sant'Angelo.
From the airport: take the Alibus shuttle towards Naples Garibaldi - Stazione Centrale, then use Metro Line 2 as above. At certain times, you can also take the direct bus 180 towards Piazzale Tecchio with a normal bus ticket.
Taxi from the airport: 20–30 minutes depending on traffic, approximately €25–30. Ask the driver for: “Università Federico II, Dipartimento di Matematica, Monte Sant'Angelo, via Cintia”.

Free Afternoon Suggestions

Napoli sotterranea: 40-meter underground tour of including a Greek-Roman theatre, a World War II shelter, and more (booking recommended).
Castel Sant'Elmo e Museo del Novecento a Napoli: castle dominating Naples and small museum of XXth century artists in Naples (last entrance to the museum at 4pm).
National Archeological Museum of Naples: wide ranging collection of artifacts from Greek, Roman and Renaissance times.
Herculaneum Archeological Park: one of the two famous towns buried by the 79 AD eruption of Mount Vesuvius.
Cloister of St Clare: small quiet cloister and museum in the center of Naples.
Cappella Sansevero: small chapel famous for the Veiled Christ (booking recommended).
Vesuvius National Park: multiple trails, including one around the 'Gran Cono' (the crater).
Procida is the closest island, and the hydrofoil from the port takes only 40 minutes (careful planning is required).
Capodimonte Museum and Royal Wood: the museum is closed on Wednesday, but the park remains open all week.
Further attractions closed on Wednesday: Galleria Borbonica and the catacombs of Rione Sanità.
